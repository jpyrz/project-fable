# Project Fable engagement roadmap

## The loop we should build next

The strongest next feature is **Companion Expeditions**:

1. Care for and feed a companion.
2. Choose a 10, 20, or 30 minute expedition with a visible reward theme.
3. Food traits and the companion's current mood influence a small bonus, never basic eligibility.
4. Return to one quick choice or discovery scene.
5. Earn materials, collection finds, recipe fragments, and occasional cosmetics.
6. Craft or equip the reward, update the public showcase, wishlist missing pieces, and trade duplicates.

This makes existing features reinforce one another instead of behaving like separate destinations. It also creates anticipation and a reason to return without punishing streak loss or hiding the odds.

## Recommended delivery order

### Now: repair the core feedback

- Needs decay at approximately 24 Tummy, 12 Joy, and 8 Sparkle per day, with a safe floor of 20.
- Feeding consumes a player-selected food from the bag.
- Food quality restores different amounts; surface the values before use.
- Reputation shows progress within the current level and explains its unlocks.
- Public Keeper profiles make companions, collection highlights, and wishlists shareable.
- Major celebrations remain visible for six seconds; minor celebrations remain for 4.2 seconds and can always be dismissed.

### Next: ship the expedition vertical slice

- Three expedition locations, each with its own material and collectible pool.
- Three durations and one expedition slot per account.
- Six short return scenes with two choices each.
- Twelve new finds, three recipes, one collection badge, and two cosmetics.
- Three starter food traits: **Cozy** (small Joy bonus), **Keen** (small material bonus), and **Lucky** (small collectible chance bonus).
- Explicit reward ranges and daily caps.

### Then: identity and collection depth

- Player-selected showcase slots rather than newest-items-only.
- Collection sets with visible missing silhouettes and completion badges.
- Companion likes/dislikes that create dialogue and a modest bonus, not a punishment.
- Weekly goals that can be completed on any days during the week.
- Reputation unlock preview: next recipe, market slot, profile flair, or cosmetic.
- Profile reactions using a small curated sticker set; no free-form comments initially.

## What reputation should do

Reputation is account-wide progression. It should unlock capability and identity, not raw economic dominance.

- Every level: one additional marketplace listing slot, up to a sensible cap.
- Selected levels: new recipes and expedition locations.
- Milestones: profile frames, titles, badges, palettes, and other cosmetic rewards.
- UI always shows total level, progress to the next level, and the next concrete unlock.

## What to borrow structurally, not literally

Neopets' durable systems connect games and currency to food/toys, NPC and player shops, collections, social groups, auctions/trading, customization, and recurring quests. Its current release notes also emphasize daily/weekly quests, rotating reward pools, wishlists, and try-on flows. Project Fable should use the structural lesson—systems become engaging when rewards circulate into collection and identity—without copying names, art, interfaces, lore, or exact mechanics.

## Features to keep later

Combat, guilds, auctions, direct trades, housing, breeding, and multiple towns all add depth, but none fixes the current missing loop as efficiently as expeditions plus showcase progression. Re-evaluate them after expedition retention and economy data are available.
